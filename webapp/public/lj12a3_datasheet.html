RVM Integration — Hardware Role & Signal Logic
The LJ12A3-4-Z/BX is flush-mounted beneath the Smart RVM scanning chute, positioned at a 4mm offset from the expected item travel path. When a waste item enters the classification zone, the sensor's inductive field detects the magnetic permeability change caused by metallic elements (aluminum cans), generating an active-LOW NPN pulse on the black output wire. This signal is read by the Arduino Mega's digital input to distinguish aluminum cans from PET plastic bottles in real-time, routing the servo gate accordingly. The rear RED LED pulses with each detection event for visual debug confirmation.

Application Notes

Metal Segregation Logic

Differentiates aluminum cans from PET bottles at the point of entry. LOW output = metal detected = aluminum sorting path activated.

NPN Pull-Up Requirement

Connect a 10kΩ pull-up resistor from the BLACK output wire to Vcc (+5V) when interfacing with Arduino digital input for reliable HIGH/LOW switching.

Flush vs. Non-Flush Mounting

Flush-mount: Embed fully in metal panel (no side-lobe interference). Non-flush: 3× Sn spacing from adjacent metal surfaces required.

Vibration & Shock Resistance

Rated for 10–55 Hz vibration environments and 10G shock pulses. Suitable for dynamic mechanical RVM cabinet environments.

Absolute Maximum Ratings
⚠ Do NOT exceed 36V DC supply. Do NOT reverse polarity — no internal protection diode. Short-circuit protection is NOT included; always use current-limiting resistors or fuses. Operating beyond 70°C ambient will permanently degrade sensing accuracy.
